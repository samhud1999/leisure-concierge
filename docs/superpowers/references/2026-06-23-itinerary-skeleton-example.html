Saturday · 27 June
Beach Town & Surf Culture
14° / 9° · Mostly sunny · Best day to be outdoors
  • 9:30
    Torquay Front Beach & foreshore playground
    Sheltered, calm water and a big playground right on the Esplanade. Rug up — sunny but crisp.
  • 11:00
    Short stroll on the Surf Coast Walk
    Easy, pram-friendly section along the clifftops to Point Danger lookout for big ocean views.
  • 12:30
    Fish & chips lunch by the water
    Classic Torquay takeaway on the foreshore lawns, or a warm cafe if the wind picks up.
  • 2:00
    Australian National Surfing Museum
    Hands-on displays kids love. Family pass approx $25. A great warm indoor option for the afternoon.
  • 3:30
    Surf City precinct browse
    Wander the surf brand stores next to the museum, then head back for downtime before dinner.
Beach Museum Easy walk
Tip: Mornings are calmest on the coast in winter. Pack beanies and a thermos — the museum is the perfect afternoon fallback if it clouds over.

Day drive Lighthouse Wildlife

Know Before You Go
  • Winter packing: beanies, waterproof jackets, warm layers and closed shoes. Pool swimwear stays useful — the indoor pool is heated.
  • Daylight: in late June the sun sets around 5:15pm, so plan outdoor activities for the morning and early afternoon.
  • Book ahead: confirm opening hours for Tiger Moth World and the Surfing Museum, as winter timetables can be shorter.
  • On-site backups: indoor pool, games room, day spa and the restaurant mean a wet day at the resort is never a wasted one.
  • Driving: Great Ocean Road stretches are winding — allow extra time and watch for wildlife near dawn and dusk.
